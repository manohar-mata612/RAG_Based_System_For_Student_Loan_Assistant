COMPANY OVERVIEW
----------------
Prodigy Finance is an international student loan provider founded in 2007. It offers loans to postgraduate international students without requiring collateral or a co-signer (for most eligible profiles). Prodigy Finance assesses loan eligibility based on the student's FUTURE EARNING POTENTIAL rather than past credit history or local credit scores.

Prodigy Finance supports applicants from 120+ countries and has partnered with 1,500+ schools worldwide. Loans are offered to eligible borrowers and governed by English law.

INTEREST RATE STRUCTURE
------------------------
- Variable interest rate (can move up or down with market conditions)
- Made up of two components:
  1. Fixed margin: Based on the student's profile and stays constant over the loan
  2. Benchmark rate: A market rate (e.g., SOFR – Secured Overnight Financing Rate) that fluctuates
- APR (Annual Percentage Rate): Reflects the all-inclusive cost of borrowing, including interest AND mandatory fees
- When comparing lenders, use APR (not just the stated interest rate) and read the representative example

REPAYMENT TERMS
---------------
- Repayments begin after a grace period (after program completion + grace window)
- Initial in-school period: Reduced payment of USD 100/month for the first 30 months (includes in-school and initial post-graduation period)
- Full repayment: USD 573.36/month for the remaining 150 months
- Total repayment period: 180 months (15 years) in the representative example
- No prepayment penalties (you can repay faster if you want to reduce interest over time)
- Co-signer product (Indian students): Includes in-school payments designed to reduce the amount that accrues, lowering the total cost over time
- Federal loans and federal income-driven repayment plans: NOT available to most international students; these are restricted to U.S. citizens and eligible noncitizens

ELIGIBLE EXPENSES (WHAT LOAN COVERS)

Funds are paid directly to the university. The school confirms the CoA and enrolment.

APPLICATION PROCESS (STEP BY STEP)
------------------------------------
Step 1: Apply online
- Register an account on Prodigy Finance
- Submit applications for any supported schools
- Eligibility check verifies: Is the university supported? Is the study country supported? Is the applicant from a supported country?
- Provisional quote available even before program acceptance (proof of admission required before finalising the loan)

Step 2: Get your offer
- Receive a non-binding provisional offer valid for 14 days
- Offer is personalised based on profile, programme, and chosen school
- Rate can vary based on student's profile, programme, and school

Step 3: Document verification
- Accept your offer and upload supporting documents
- If successfully verified, you will be matched to available funding (called "matching")

Step 4: Loan confirmation
- Once funding is allocated, accept the offer and pay the USD 500 processing fee to finalise the loan
- The USD 500 processing fee is the only upfront fee required

Step 5: Complete final steps (after arriving on campus)
- Monitor your Prodigy dashboard and emails for additional tasks needed before finalising the loan agreement

Step 6: Sign your loan agreement
- Sign the loan agreement digitally once on campus
- Funds are then sent directly to the school

HOW VARIABLE INTEREST RATES WORK
----------------------------------
- Fixed margin: Set based on profile, stays the same throughout the loan
- Benchmark rate: Based on a market rate such as SOFR; can move up or down with market conditions
- Total rate = Fixed margin + Benchmark rate
- APR shows the all-in cost of borrowing including interest AND mandatory fees
- When rates go up, your total interest cost increases; when they go down, your total interest cost decreases

REPAYMENT PLANNING TIPS
------------------------
- Borrow only what you need (map tuition, rent, food, health insurance, arrival costs)
- Compare APRs and fees, not just interest rates
- Plan for the first 3 to 6 months after graduation (job hunting and relocation buffer)
- Use early repayments: Even small voluntary payments during study can reduce total cost
- If eligible for co-signer option (Indian students), compare projected total cost and monthly payments over the full term

COMPARISON: FEDERAL VS PRIVATE LOANS (FOR INTERNATIONAL STUDENTS)
-------------------------------------------------------------------
Federal loans:
- Generally NOT available to international students who are not "eligible noncitizens"
- Income-driven repayment plans and federal forgiveness programmes do NOT apply to international students in most cases

Private loans (Prodigy Finance):
- Available to international students
- No collateral or co-signer required at supported schools
- Assessed on school/programme outcomes and future earning potential
- Variable interest rate (APR reflects all costs including fees)

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)
----------------------------------

Q: Who is eligible for a Prodigy Finance student loan?
A: International postgraduate students admitted to supported programmes at eligible schools. Prodigy Finance supports students from 120+ countries. Eligibility is based on future earning potential, not local credit history.

Q: What are the loan repayment terms?
A: Repayments begin after a grace period following program completion. The representative example shows initial payments of USD 100/month for 30 months, followed by USD 573.36/month for 150 months (180 months total / 15 years). No prepayment penalties apply.

Q: Can I apply without a co-signer or collateral?
A: Yes. The standard Prodigy Finance product requires NO co-signer and NO collateral. Eligibility is assessed based on your profile, programme, and the earning potential associated with your chosen school and degree.

Q: Can I repay my loan early?
A: Yes. There are no prepayment penalties. Making early payments (even during school) can reduce your total cost.

Q: Does Prodigy Finance pay me or my school?
A: Prodigy Finance sends funds directly to your school (university), not to you personally. The school confirms your cost of attendance and enrolment first.

Q: When should I apply for the UK Student visa?
A: This depends on the specific visa process for the UK; consult official government guidance. Prodigy Finance provides general guidance but is not authorised to provide immigration advice.

Q: How early can I apply for a US student visa?
A: This depends on U.S. immigration rules; consult official government guidance. Prodigy Finance provides general guidance but is not authorised to provide immigration advice.

Q: When can I apply for OPT?
A: OPT (Optional Practical Training) is a U.S.-specific immigration work authorisation. Consult USCIS for detailed eligibility and timing. Prodigy Finance provides general guidance but is not an immigration adviser.

Q: What happens if my visa expires while I'm in the US?
A: This is an immigration matter. Consult USCIS or a qualified immigration attorney immediately.

Q: When should I apply for a study permit in Canada?
A: This depends on Canadian immigration processing timelines. Consult IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada) for official guidance.
