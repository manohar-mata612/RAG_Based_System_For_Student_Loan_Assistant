REPAYMENT TERMS
---------------
- Students make INTEREST-ONLY payments while enrolled in school AND for the six months following graduation
- After the six-month grace period, full principal + interest repayment begins
- Common repayment term: approximately 10 years
- No prepayment penalty (you can pay off early without being charged)
- Building U.S. credit history through on-time payments is a key benefit
- On-time interest payments during school build credit history before full repayment begins

MORATORIUM / GRACE PERIOD
---------------------------
- Interest-only payments required during school and for 6 months after graduation
- After the 6-month grace period, full repayment (principal + interest) begins
- These interest-only payments allow the student to focus on earning a degree and getting a job before entering full repayment

WHO IS ELIGIBLE
---------------
- International students (non-U.S. citizens/non-permanent residents) enrolled at an eligible U.S. or Canadian school
- School must be on MPOWER's list of 500+ eligible schools
- Student must be within 2 years of graduating (junior, senior, or graduate student) at time of application for U.S. schools; or enrolled at an eligible Canadian school
- Citizens of 150+ countries are eligible
- Must be accepted into an eligible school before applying
- No cosigner or U.S./Canadian credit history required
- Federal student loans are NOT available to international students (those are for U.S. citizens and eligible noncitizens only); MPOWER fills this gap

SCHOOL ELIGIBILITY

WHAT LOANS CAN BE USED FOR
---------------------------
For U.S. schools: Loan funds cover all eligible school-related expenses (tuition, fees, housing, meal plans, health insurance, books, supplies, living expenses)
For Canadian schools: Loans can be used for tuition and university-invoiced expenses
- Schools send the remaining amount (after institutional costs) to the student for books, supplies, and living expenses
- School must certify (approve) the loan amount
- Best practice: Only borrow what you need

HOW MUCH TO BORROW
-------------------
- Look at the full cost of attendance (not just tuition): room and board, books and supplies, general living expenses
- Some financial experts recommend borrowing no more than the amount you expect to earn in your first year after graduating
- Example: If entry-level salary expected is US$35,000, keep student loan borrowing at US$35,000 or less
- MPOWER minimum: US$2,001 per academic period
- MPOWER maximum: US$50,000 per academic period
- MPOWER lifetime total: US$100,000

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQ)
----------------------------------

Q: What is an international student loan?
A: An international student loan is a private education loan designed for international students studying at universities in the U.S. International students are NOT eligible for federal student loans (reserved for U.S. citizens and eligible noncitizens such as permanent residents).

Q: Am I eligible for an international student loan?
A: Every lender sets its own requirements. Most want to see enrollment in an eligible university. Many U.S. lenders also require a cosigner who is a U.S. citizen or permanent resident with good credit and income. MPOWER Financing offers loans without a cosigner or collateral.

Q: How do I get a student loan without a cosigner?
A: MPOWER Financing offers student loans for international students with no cosigner or collateral required. You can borrow money for college or graduate school without having to ask anyone else to share your debt.

Q: My school is not on MPOWER's list. Can I still get a loan?
A: If your school is not currently on MPOWER's list of 500+ eligible schools, you would not be able to apply at this time. Check back periodically to see if your school has been added.

Q: What can international student loans be used for?
A: Loans cover the costs of university including tuition, fees, housing, meal plans, health insurance, books, supplies, and other living expenses.

Q: Do I need to start paying back my student loan right away?
A: No. MPOWER requires interest-only payments while in school and for the six months following graduation. Full repayment begins after the 6-month grace period.

Q: How long does it take to pay back a student loan?
A: A common term is 10 years. You can make extra payments to pay off faster without a prepayment penalty.

Q: Can I apply for an international student loan before being accepted to my school?
A: Most lenders, including MPOWER, require you to be accepted into an eligible school before applying. The acceptance letter serves as proof of enrollment.

Q: Can international students refinance their student loans?
A: Yes. MPOWER offers refinancing for international students without requiring a cosigner, allowing students to secure better rates and repayment terms.

Q: Are there loan forgiveness programs for international students?
A: Loan forgiveness programs are generally limited to domestic students and are typically offered by government agencies. International students usually do not have access to these programs.

Q: What happens if I can't repay my international student loan?
A: Contact MPOWER immediately to discuss available options. Acting promptly can mitigate adverse credit reporting or late fees.

Q: Can I work while studying to pay off my loan?
A: Yes. In Canada, students with a valid study permit can generally work up to 24 hours per week during academic sessions and full time during scheduled breaks. In the U.S., F-1 visa students can work up to 20 hours per week on campus during academic sessions and full time during scheduled breaks. Off-campus employment via OPT and CPT is available under certain conditions.

Q: I need proof of funds before I can enroll; can I use loans?
A: Yes. MPOWER provides loan acceptance letters you can use to demonstrate you have sufficient funds to cover tuition and living expenses. This is often a requirement for obtaining a study permit or visa.
